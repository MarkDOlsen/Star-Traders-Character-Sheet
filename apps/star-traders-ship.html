Quick Reference

Ship's Sensors

A ship sensor's can gather basic information about anything not blocked by a planet, star, stealth systems, or interfering stellar phenomena, within the system it is in without needing a check.

This includes things like: number, location, and size of planets; basic planetary atmosphere of each planet; presence of active beacons; presence and intensity of ship to ship or ship to planet communications; number, location (as a point source only), speed and heading of any other ships in the system.

Gathering more detailed information requires a Systems check with a base difficulty depending on the type of information the person at the sensors is trying to gather, with a +1 per transit zone between the ship and anything it is trying to gather information on.

Ship Combat

Ship combat happens in phases. In each phase one person takes one of the listed actions.

  1. Command Phase (Captain)
    Initiative. At the start of the exchange, roll Notice or Systems for initiative.
    Command Presence. Make Command or Rapport checks to create advantages around giving the crew orders, leadership, and moral boosts. The captain can assist with, or make, checks with Systems or Piloting instead of creating advantages.
  2. Piloting Phase (Pilot)
    Evade. Ships can take evasive maneuvers at the cost of 1 zone of movement. The pilot makes a Pilot roll and that is defense against any incoming attacks that exchange. If a pilot does not take evasive maneuvers in an exchange then their ship has a passive resistance of +0 against attacks that exchange.
    Maneuver. Move 1 zone per exchange (2 for Engine 2 or higher). The pilot can attempt a Pilot roll to move more zones (+4 for one additional zone, +6 for two additional zones). Ships can change their facing as part of their movement, up to 45 degrees at the cost of 1 zone of movement.
  3. Guns Phase (Gunner Stations)
    Fire Weapons! Each weapon a ship has requires a gunner. Gunners pick their targets and roll Shoot. Beam weapons hit their target immediately. Missile weapons hit their target at the start of the next exchange.

Emergency Ship Repairs

During missions, starships often sustain damage. While permanent repairs can be made with an Engineering roll and spare parts (which cost credits), engineers can perform temporary repairs during combat to keep the ship operational.

These temporary fixes create a TEMPORARY REPAIRS aspect, which the GM can compel for free once per subsequent fight until proper repairs are completed.

To make repairs mid-combat, an engineer must use their action to make an Engineering roll against the damaged condition's difficulty. A success clears one stress box on that condition; a success with style clears both.

Engineering can also stabilize disastrous consequences (like a HULL BREACH) to prevent them from being compelled during the current conflict. Stabilizing a mild consequence requires a Fair (+2) Engineering roll, a moderate consequence requires a Great (+4) roll, and a severe consequence requires a Fantastic (+6) roll.
