Welcome

What kind of game can you expect from me?

Welcome! If you’re considering joining one of my campaigns, this page should give you a clear picture of who I am as a Game Master, the kinds of stories I like to tell, and what you can expect when you sit down at my table.

I have been playing tabletop RPGs since 1979, and over the decades I have played hundreds of systems and run games across a wide range of genres and styles. While I have worn many hats over the years—Dungeon Master, Referee, Storyteller, and more—I prefer the term Game Master. My goal is to use that experience to help create a smooth, immersive, and exciting experience for everyone involved.

In short: I aim to run games that are atmospheric, player-driven, dramatic, and challenging, while still being fair, collaborative, and fun.

Tone & Atmosphere

Pulp sword & sorcery, mystery, menace, and adventure.

While I run many genres, my baseline approach is rooted in classic fantasy. I am heavily inspired by old-school sword and sorcery pulp stories—think Conan the Barbarian, Elric of Melniboné, and Fafhrd and the Gray Mouser—mixed with the epic strangeness of The Lord of the Rings and The Book of the New Sun.

  • Mystery and Menace: I like worlds filled with ancient ruins, strange powers, half-lost histories, and a constant sense that civilization is holding back something older, darker, or less understandable.
  • Cinematic Horror: My settings often include Lovecraftian elements, eerie imagery, and vivid descriptions of danger and violence. I enjoy atmosphere and tension, but I generally stop short of full grimdark hopelessness.
  • Pulp Action: I favor the Rule of Cool, but I like it grounded in the logic of the setting. I want dramatic swashbuckling action, desperate escapes, and memorable heroic risks that still feel believable within the world.

My GMing Style

Emergent storytelling over pre-written plot.

I do not think of myself as an author writing a novel for the players to perform. I think of myself as a facilitator presenting a world, its dangers, and its opportunities, and then responding honestly to what the players choose to do.

  • Sandbox Agency: I do not like railroads or heavily scripted plotlines. I prefer open-ended campaigns built around factions, rumors, opportunities, and complications that the players choose to pursue.
  • The World Is Alive: I rarely plan more than a session or two ahead. The setting exists beyond the player characters, and events may evolve, worsen, or resolve based on your choices—or your inaction.
  • Logical Challenge: Challenges are based on the internal logic of the world, not on a carefully curated “balanced encounter” model. I try to telegraph danger clearly, but sometimes the smartest choice really is to retreat.

Rules & Rulings

Flexible systems, quick decisions, and creative problem-solving.

I tend to prefer flexible, rules-light systems—especially Fate—because they support improvisation, collaboration, and character-driven play.

  • Rulings Over Rules: If an unusual situation comes up that is not clearly covered by the rules, I will make a common-sense ruling and keep the game moving. I care more about fairness, consistency, and momentum than strict page-flipping.
  • Theater of the Mind: I usually run scenes and combat through verbal description rather than battlemats and miniatures. I care more about relative position, intent, and momentum than exact square counting.
  • Player Ingenuity: If you come up with a clever approach that is not written on your character sheet, I want to hear it. Creative play is one of my favorite parts of the hobby.

Your Role as a Player

Proactive characters, collaborative play, and shared momentum.

  • The Pulp Hero: I look for characters who are larger-than-life adventurers with personal goals, strong motivations, and a reason to engage with danger. They do not need to be saints or world-savers, but they should not be villains either.
  • Roleplaying, Not Performing: I love immersion, but I do not require accents, in-character monologues, or theatrical performance. First-person roleplay is welcome, but third-person description is just as welcome.
  • The Group Comes First: I strongly discourage power-building for its own sake. Build the character you want to play, and I will build adventures that give that character interesting things to do. Most importantly, your character must be willing to work with the party. I have a zero-tolerance policy for PvP conflict.
  • Metagaming: I expect us to interact like adults. If you learn something at the table that your character does not know, I trust you to play honestly. I generally lean in the players’ favor, but I do not want out-of-game assumptions to replace in-game judgment.

Safety and Comfort

Dark themes, clear boundaries, and a table built on trust.

Because my games often include elements of cosmic horror, graphic violence, and macabre imagery, I prioritize player safety. I use Lines and Veils and related safety tools to make sure we can explore dark themes without crossing personal boundaries.

If something becomes too intense or uncomfortable, we pause, adjust, and move forward. My goal is for the game to be thrilling, immersive, and challenging—but always fun and respectful for everyone involved.

My Gaming History

Decades in the hobby, across a lot of different systems.

I have been playing tabletop RPGs for a long time and have most often been the Dungeon Master, Referee, Storyteller, or Game Master—which is the term I prefer.

I started back in 1979 with the Dungeons & Dragons Basic Set (BX), although Gamma World was technically the first game I ever played. In the early 1980s I moved into BECMI D&D, mixed with Advanced Dungeons & Dragons, and those early years of just figuring things out with no one to explain them to us are a big part of what made me love the hobby.
